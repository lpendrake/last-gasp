# **Campaign Arc: The Twin Thorns Investigation**

**Premise:** A secret, radicalized cult combining the faiths of **Milani** (Revolution) and **Calistria** (Revenge) is destabilizing Stormhaven. They are led by **"Father" Julian Vane**, who is actually **Xylanthros**, an adult Conspirator Dragon feeding on the chaos.

## **1\. The Hook: A Romantic Interrogation**

**Scene:** The player (Hellknight/Champion) is invited by **Inquisitor Seraphina Vane** to a private "dinner date" in the interrogation cells of the Temple of Asmodeus. The ambiance is candlelight and the screams of a prisoner in the background.

**The Prisoner:** A terrified, low-level agitator caught vandalizing a statue of Abadar with red paint. He is not a core cultist, just a sympathizer.

**The Reveal:** Between courses of the meal and turns of the thumbscrew, the prisoner breaks. He reveals:

* **The Name:** "The Twin Thorns."  
* **The Symbol:** A minimalist glyph consisting of three rapid strokes: a spiral for the rose and a jagged line striking through it. It is designed to be scratched instantly into stone or daubed in blood.  
* **The Chant:** "Weeds strangle the Rose. The Gardener must prune the rot."  
* **The Intel:** He doesn't know where they meet, only that they are planning "The Pruning." He mentions seeing a list of names, but he only remembers seeing the seal of the **smokers** next to one and a **Judge's gavel** next to another before he was shooed away.

**Seraphina's Gift:** She wipes her hands on a napkin, kisses the player on the cheek, and hands them the file. "I have more pressing matters with the high council. This rabble is beneath me, but perfect for a man of your... direct talents. Consider it a gift. Find them, crush them, and bring me their leader's head."

***GM Secret:** Seraphina does not yet know the cult leader's face. If she were to see him, she would recognize him immediately as her father, **Lord Julian Vane**, who went missing years ago. She has exhausted all resources trying to find him, unaware that Xylanthros killed him and stole his form.*

## **2\. The Breadcrumbs (Three Events)**

The player must investigate three seemingly isolated incidents. Individually, they look like random crimes. Together, they reveal the pattern.

### **Event A: The "Accidental" Death of Magistrate Havelock**

* **The Incident:** Magistrate Havelock (a stern Asmodean judge known for harsh sentencing) was found dead in his study. The official report says "heart failure."  
* **The Clue (Medicine/Investigation):** Upon closer inspection of the body (perhaps at the Pharasman morgue or the crime scene), the player finds a tiny, almost invisible puncture wound on the neck.  
* **The Link:** A DC 15 Nature/Crafting check identifies the toxin not as a natural poison, but a synthetic blend of **giant wasp venom** and a paralytic agent derived from **mutated roses** found only near the Blight.  
* **The False Lead:** Witnesses swear they saw Havelock arguing with his own clerk hours before death. (In reality, the Dragon Xylanthros shifted into the clerk's form to get close, then shifted away).

### **Event B: The Sabotage of "The Iron Lung"**

* **The Incident:** A massive air-filtration bellows in the primary Smoker foundry, nicknamed "The Iron Lung," exploded, killing three overseers and halting production of steel for the city guard.  
* **The Clue (Engineering/Crafting):** The explosion wasn't a boiler failure. The gears were jammed with a strange, fibrous material.  
* **The Link:** Pulling the debris apart reveals the material is **petrified rose stems**, hard as iron. Scratched into the main casing of the machine is the phrase: *"Let them breathe the ash they create."*  
* **The Connection:** This targets the **Smokers** faction leadership and infrastructure, mirroring the attack on the **Asmodean** judge.

### **Event C: The Vanishing of Captain Thorne's Tribute**

* **The Incident:** A House of Storms courier carrying a massive bribe (framed as a "tribute") intended for a high-ranking Free Sails captain was intercepted. The gold wasn't stolen; it was melted down into a slag heap in the middle of the street.  
* **The Clue (Society/Streetwise):** A beggar witness (one of the player's spies) saw the attack. They claim a man matching the description of **"Father Julian Vane"** led the attack.  
* **The Anomaly:** The beggar is confused because they *also* saw Father Vane giving a sermon in the Silent City at the exact same time. They assume they are going mad.  
* **The Truth (GM Secret):** The "Father Vane" giving the sermon was a **body double**—a devoted Changeling lieutenant named **Oryn** wearing a mundane disguise. Xylanthros uses Oryn to handle public relations and establish iron-clad alibis while he commits the actual crimes in his "Vane" persona (or other disguises).  
* **The Link:** The melted gold forms a crude shape on the cobblestones—a broken chain. This targets the **House of Storms/Free Sails** alliance.

## **3\. The Realization: The Pattern of "The Pruning"**

When the player puts these three events together, the pattern becomes clear. The Twin Thorns isn't just causing random riots; they are systematically "pruning" the pillars of Stormhaven's stability.

1. **Magistrate Havelock:** Represents **Law/Judiciary** (Asmodeus).  
2. **The Iron Lung:** Represents **Industry/Economy** (Smokers).  
3. **The Melted Tribute:** Represents **Trade/Naval Power** (House of Storms).

**The Conclusion:** The cult isn't trying to take over; they are trying to decapitate the society to induce total anarchy.

**The Draconic Hint:** The impossible alibi in Event C (Julian being in two places) is the first subtle hint to the Hellknight that their enemy is not merely a charismatic priest, but something that can wear faces like masks—a **Conspirator Dragon**.

## **4\. The Antagonist Profile: Xylanthros (aka Father Julian Vane)**

* **True Form:** Adult Conspirator Dragon (Lawful Evil, but acts Chaotic to sow discord).  
* **Disguise:** **"Father" Julian Vane**, appearing as Inquisitor Seraphina Vane's long-lost father. He is an elderly, distinguished, yet weary-looking human revolutionary.  
* **Motivation:** Xylanthros feeds on the metaphysical weight of **conspiracy and social collapse**. A standard revolution is too simple; he wants a complex, multi-faction civil war where nobody knows who to trust. He uses the *Twin Thorns* to weaponize the legitimate grievances of the Milanites and the petty spite of the Calistrians, twisting them until they destroy the city that feeds them.  
* **Tactics:**  
  * **Polymorph:** Uses his *Change Shape* ability to impersonate key figures, creating distrust (e.g., impersonating an Asmodean guard to beat a citizen, then impersonating a Milanite rebel to kill a guard).  
  * **Memory Manipulation:** If caught, he uses magic to alter the witness's memory, making them believe they saw someone else.  
  * **Breath Weapon:** poisonous fog that he can use to escape or assassinate groups.
