# City of Stormhaven on the the Island of Emberheart

## Laws

1. Respect the Accords  
   1. The accords were agreed to by all factions and the Asmodean and Abadarian factions were trusted to enforce it and police each other.  
   2. The factions can challenge the temple’s rulings or actions by gathering all the faction leaders.  
   3. Abuse of this power can result in forced removal from the charter unless the faction selects a new leader.  
2. No Killing  
   1. Even legal execution is frowned upon  
3. Regulated Harm  
   1. Duels, sparring, arena matches. Except for sparing, a representative of the law must confirm with each participant their willingness and free will.  
   2. The severity of the harm, not the intent, is what matters.  
4. No theft  
   1. Theft is not permitted.   
   2. The resources of the island are vast but acquiring them carries great risk. Theft is tantamount to inflicting said risk on the victim.  
      1. As such, stealing a fish is not very serious, but stealing the tooth of a cave troll or a plant only found far from town would be quite serious.  
   3. The rarity factors in as well, so a rare gem is a more serious theft than a lump of common unworked iron.  
5. Honour the Contract  
   1. Your word is your bond  
   2. Breaking your word is a punishable offence  
6. Preserve Infrastructure  
   1. The city’s wellbeing is paramount. Damage to it’s infrastructure will be taken seriously  
7. Trespassing  
   1. It’s a thing. Don’t.  
8. Mind Controlling Magic  
   1. After a psychic and a wizard magically manipulated a significant number of officials, mind control magic became a regulated ability.  
   2. Unlicensed use of mind control magic is an offense   
9. Control of Dangerous Creatures  
   1. The island is home to many fascinating creatures.  
   2. Bringing them into town requires a permit  
10. Proper Burial Rights  
    1. A zombie outbreak after the first conflict led to a rule where all corpses must be sactified before burial, or disposed of in such a way as to not be a potential threat.  
11. Exploitation of Resources  
    1. Renewable resources such as lumber, fish, wild meat is regulated by the church of Erastil  
    2. Exploitation of these resources is punishable

## The Free Sails

The Free Sails are a diverse group of sailors, pirates, merchants, and explorers who found themselves stranded on the island when the magical barrier rose. United by their shared experience and a desire for freedom, they formed a unique community aboard their ships.

They have the biggest alcohol production business. They also provide a large amount of food for the island in the form of fish and extract protection money for anyone who wants to be a fisherman without joining them. Not many choose that option though.

The alchemist Captain Switftwind also provides a number of drugs and alchemical solutions via a small group of alchemists he has working on the far side of the island, in secret.

## The Smokers

So called because of the smog that the steam powered machines, smelting and forging produce.

They are A group of goblins, orks, hobgoblins and other often vilified species who were blamed when the barrier went up. They banded together for protection and have claimed to be innocent of triggering the barrier.

Ironically, they have one of the most free societies on the island because they feel falsely accused and their leader is more smart than charismatic and takes a very hands off approach. This makes them generally very hard to make deals with because he refuses to represent the group as a whole unless they are threatened.

Their leader is a hobgoblin inventor who was hired to help build some industry on the island.

As such, he has guided the Smokers to dominate the metal and wood production of the island.

# Ancients

## History Of

* An ancient, advanced civilization on the verge of extinction.  
* Relied heavily on cloning technology to prolong their lives.  
* Centuries of cloning has led to the clones becoming weaker and less stable.  
* The Ancients then started experimenting with mind transfer to try and survive.  
* This cloning process creates mindless "vessels" as the new bodies.  
* The process of transferring the mind also required transferring the soul to avoid insanityl but their attempts to do so on mass, cheaply, damaged the souls.  
  * This damage didn’t become visible for several generations and that’s when the research started.  
  * Peering into souls is hard and took several more generations to even identify, let alone understand the source.  
  * They never found a way to stop the damage but did refine their methods, slowing the progress.  
  * They then setup on this island and experimented on creatures sentient and otherwise, dealt with fiends and otherworldly entities, though never god tier entities, they were die hard atheists.  
    * Recognising the potential disaster of soul damaged clone monsters roaming the world, they setup the island like a prison, with several failsafes to ensure no escapes including a huge teleport block, and a now lost magic that allowed them to isolate the island. The fog and storm surrounding the island is a side effect.  
  * Eventually they summoned a creature from between the stars. It was intelligent but ultimately uncooperative.  
    * It’s ability to teleport at will is the reason for the smaller teleportation barriers.  
  * When they cloned it and tried to experiment on transference of it’s soul, they found no resulting damage.  
  * They repeated the process countless times, monitoring the results, studying the clones.  
  * Eventually an elder of this creatures race, having heard the pleas of it’s weaker kin, showed up on galorian.   
  * This enormous creature waged a bloody war on their kind, their numbers dwindling and eventually it found the island.  
  * They anticipated this and essentially turned the island into a giant trap for the creature.  
    * It worked, and was trapped in one of the mountains, though the concentration of it’s power and the traps power turned the mountain into a volcano.  
  * They studied the creature as much as they could without risking it’s release
