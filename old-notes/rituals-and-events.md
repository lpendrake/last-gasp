# Rituals and Events

# Abadar

## The Ledger's Blessing (Early Spring): 

* This event marks the start of the fiscal year. Abadaran priests bless ledgers, contracts, and business tools, praying for prosperity and fair dealings. It's a time for merchants to renew their vows of ethical trade and for the community to review its financial health.  
* Ties to: Spring's new beginnings, planning for the year.

## The City's Foundation (Mid-Spring): 

* A celebration of the founding of Stormhaven (or whatever specific city), emphasizing the importance of infrastructure, community building, and law and order. Citizens participate in public works projects, and the city's legal codes are reaffirmed.  
* Ties to: Building, community, law and order.

## The Golden Harvest (Late Summer/Early Autumn): 

* A harvest festival celebrating the bounty of the land and the success of trade. Abadaran priests bless the harvested crops and the goods produced by the city's artisans. It's a time for feasting, bartering, and giving thanks for prosperity.  
* Ties to: Harvest season, economic success.

## The Scales of Justice (Autumn Equinox): 

* A day of reflection on the laws and their enforcement. Abadaran priests hold public hearings, where citizens can voice grievances and seek redress. It's a time for settling disputes and reaffirming the principles of fairness and impartiality.  
* Ties to: Equinox, balance, justice.

## The Vault's Vigil (Mid-Winter): 

* A solemn ceremony held in the city's vaults or treasuries. Abadaran priests safeguard the city's wealth and pray for its protection against theft and corruption. It's a time for reflecting on the value of hard work and the importance of financial responsibility.  
* Ties to: Winter, protection of resources, planning for the future.

## The Market's Renewal (New Year's Day): 

* This festival celebrates the new year and the continuation of trade. The main market place is cleaned and rededicated to fair trade. New contracts are signed, and the people ask Abadar for his blessing on the new year's business.  
* Ties to: New year, new beginnings, trade.

# Asmodeus

## The Binding of the Weak (Early Spring): 

* This event focuses on reaffirming the social hierarchy. Contracts of servitude and oaths of loyalty are renewed. Public displays of obedience and deference are expected. Lower-ranking members of society are reminded of their place.  
* Ties to: Spring's renewal, social order, control.

## The Trial of Ambition (Mid-Spring): 

* Individuals who have demonstrated exceptional ambition or cunning are publicly recognized and rewarded. However, they are also subjected to rigorous tests of loyalty and obedience. Those who fail are publicly humiliated or punished.  
* Ties to: Ambition, power, testing of loyalty.

### Morning \- The Proving Ground

The participant must pass two of three tests at least.

Cha \- Make a deal with a priest. The deal must be followed through, it’s not fake, the stakes are real. The priest offers the power to influence the minds of others. In exchange, he wants regular donations to the temple.  
If the deal is struck, the player receives an Asmodean Ring of Dominance.  
If the deal is refused by either party, the test is failed.  
Diplomacy results

- Crit \- The priest reveals that once 700gp of donation has been accrued, the power of the ring will grow. However, Asmodeus has empowered him to reward contestants so he will lower that to 525gp. He also reveals that breaking the item does not erase the debt. 26.25gp per week or the item stops working.   
- Success \- The priest reveals that 700gp is the total due and hints that a further reward will be granted upon completion of the payment. 35gp per week or the item stops working.  
- Failure \- The priest offers a vague deal where in the player must consent to give weekly donations of 35gp or lose the power they are being granted. He does not mention the cap but mentions that the power may grow, to entice the player.  
- Critical Failure \- Disgusted by the poor negotiation skills the priest punishes the player, asking for Weekly Donations of 43.75gp weekly and not mentioning the increased power or the cap.

Int \- A chess puzzle the participant must solve in as few moves as possible. There is only 1 right answer. Each wrong move resets the whole puzzle but also deals spirit damage to the participant who is chained to the chair. 

- The puzzle requires 6 correct moves

Failure does not mean death.  
Wis \- Three people are accused of the same crime. Each will defend themselves. At the end of the day, the one with more than 50% of the vote will be executed. Should there be a tie, both/all three will be executed. In the event that none of the previous conditions are met, all three are executed to ensure justice is served. This includes the scenario where nobody chooses this trial.

## The Festival of Infernal Contracts (Late Summer/Early Autumn): 

* This festival celebrates the power of contracts and agreements. New contracts are forged, and existing ones are renegotiated. Public displays of wealth and power are common, as individuals seek to improve their standing through advantageous deals.  
* Ties to: Harvest, negotiation, wealth.

## The Shadow Games (Autumn Equinox): 

* A series of competitive events designed to test cunning, strategy, and ruthlessness. Participants engage in games of deception, manipulation, and power plays. The winners are rewarded with prestige and influence.  
* Ties to: Equinox, balance of power, manipulation.

## The Night of Infernal Whispers (Mid-Winter): 

* A secretive event where individuals can seek favors or make pacts with Asmodean priests. Information is exchanged, and deals are struck in the shadows. It's a time for consolidating power and manipulating events from behind the scenes.  
* Ties to: Winter, secrecy, manipulation.

## The Rite of Infernal Ascension (New Year's Day): 

* This ritual reaffirms the hierarchy of the Asmodean church and society. Individuals are ranked based on their power and influence. Those who have risen in the ranks are publicly acknowledged, while those who have fallen are reminded of their diminished status.  
* Ties to: New year, hierarchy, power.
