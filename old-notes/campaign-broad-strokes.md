# The Last Gasp of Civilisation

## A group of adventurers is tasked with infiltrating a mysterious, isolated island. What they discover is a world trapped in time, a place of ancient secrets, forgotten lore, and deadly dangers. As they delve deeper into the island's dark heart, they'll uncover a terrible truth that threatens to consume them all.

I want to have it take place in a single location, such as a large town. To make it different, I'd like this to be a port town on an island filled with hostile beasties.

Tropical weather, boats, definitely some pirates though it's still a town with a semblance of law. 

The town is built on the ruins of an ancient port city of unknown origin and it's only been a few years since people arrived to settle the island.

At some point in the last few years, someone triggered an ancient defence mechanism on the island and a giant ring of rapidly swirling water appeared around the island.

The ring reaches down to the sea floor and above it there are winds that defy any sailor's ability to cross them, even if they could deal with the water ring moving at speeds that defy nature.

Teleportation magic seems to fail dramatically when used to get out, though it does seem to work getting in, trapping more unfortunates inside. Since you need to know where you're going to teleport there, there are very very few people left capable of making the jump to the island and only one that wants to take the risk of never coming back.

The Hook: This person is hiring the players, let's call them Alf of Gand, a wizard. The players are a few adventurers that he has hired several times before and are suicidal enough to let him take them to what appears to be certain death.

The overall story: The players are trying to figure out what is causing the ring that's cutting off the island from the world. The truth of the matter is that it's an ancient prison, hence why things can get in, but not out. Many creatures are held in stasis within the prison, others are simply long lived enough to have survived the few thousand years on the island.   
Over the millenia, other civilisations have built upon the ruins and failed. 

Structure: I want to split the story into 9 chapters, each with a hook, goal and cliffhanger or lead into the next chapter. 

# Critical Events

The events happen in the following order but the timing of them is up to the GM.

##  1 \- Volcanic Activity

- The volcano has it’s first significant eruption.  
- This leads to the campaign starting, an ancient wakes a few of his test subjects that he’s not ruined yet and attempts to implant memories into them. The players are the only survivors of this process and wake up together.  
- The volcano causes a lot of fire and earth and metal elementals to run rampant.

## 2 \- Strange creatures seen in the hills.

- This is actually mutated experiments of the ancients waking and roaming.   
- They will increase in number over time.  
- Players can gain some knowledge and reduce the occurrences by “resolving” the threat of the nearby stasis chambers full of hundreds of mutated creatures.  
- Small settlements of mutated experiments form in the wilds. None of them have clear memories of the ancients or experiments and many are aggressive beyond reason, but some might be reasoned with.

## 3 \- Strange magic corrupts the wilds

- This is actually the creature in the volcano having some wriggle room and causing the very powerful magic of it’s cage to leak out into the world around the volcano. It doesn’t care about doing this safely so the effects almost entirely negative.  
- There is 0 evidence of the actual cause, but scouts report that the effects feel stronger further from the coast.  
- Figuring out ways to hold off the magic is important  
  - Temples, the various casters, maybe even the alchemists or engineers of the city can provide solutions with the players help. Who they help shapes the form of the solution.

## 4 \- The corruption affects the city

- Now everyone cares, everyone wants to protect their own first but it won’t work, they need to cooperate.  
- Players can just leave the city to settle this but it will be the least optimal solution with the most blood spilled.  
- This is the massive conflict where players use their connections to try to unify the city and pick a solution to holding off the corruption.  
- Don’t progress to 5 until a solution to protect the city is found.  
  - If too much time passes, move to 6\.

## 5 \- The corruption causes holes in the material plane

- Pressure escalates massively if the players don’t act before the   
- Players must find the source. How? Explore the island.   
- The source is of course the creature in the volcano, but it is not that which they find, but a powerful magical building powered by a vast ritual that drains power from the volcano. The creatures thrashing about it it’s cage is destabilising the matrix that drains the volcano, thereby causing the volcanic activity that should have occurred a long time ago.  
- Though they may identify the source, what to do should be a big complex issue requiring the help of the city’s most powerful.  
- What they do notice:  
  - The matrix doesn’t just power whatever the building is, it is powering the barrier around the island.  
  - The matrix is damaged and it will only get worse.   
  - Power could be shifted from the barrier but doing so would destabilize the matrix which would likely cause the volcano to erupt.  
- If the players want to find out what is in the building, which is made of entirely smooth, iridescent metal with no entrances or windows, they can find a powerline leading to some as yet unexplored part of the island.  
  - This reveals that some sort of vastly powerful otherworldly creature is trapped in there. It was somehow fascinating to the ancients, they thought it was the answer and eventually constructed the volcano layer to summon and trap it but it was too powerful to study safely, all they could do was seal it away

## 6 \- The cult && the ancients && the players

- A cult formed during the 4th event acts to try to free the creature which they’ve managed to communicate with briefly. It’s power and desire for freedom makes them think it can break the barrier.   
  - It let them believe this, it knows that breaking free will break it’s prison, the volcanos power siphoning matrix and therefore the barrier. If these insignificant specks can help it get free sooner, that’s great.  
  - For simplicities sake, contact with the creature’s mind causes the cultists to go mad, but gifts them a great deal of power.  
  - The cult will be travelling to the five points on the island where they will conduct rituals to reverse the power from the barrier back into the volcano.   
    - Each success causes the volcano to become more active. They only need to succeed three times to destabilize the matrix and blow up the cage and the volcano  
- The ancients want to buy more time. They don’t care about the inhabitants.  
  - They want to keep the beast trapped.   
  - While everyone else is messing with the power going to the barrier, they’re rigging a ritual to plunge the cage into the volcano’s heart, making escape impossible, even if it causes the volcano to blow. They don’t mind that it’ll take decades to dig free the cage and power it again without freeing the creature, or that the volcanic eruption will wipe out all life on the island.  
  - The players likely won’t see this coming, but taking down the barrier means they can escape the eruption that is all but inevitable so it makes little difference.  
- If the players try to stop the ancients, the cult will likely succeed and then we have a lesser eruption but have to deal with the creature’s wrath.   
- If the players try to race or stop the cult, the ancients get their way, so we have a huge explosion and the city must evacuate.  
- Either way the barrier is down and the threat to the ancients, the mutants and stormhavens civilization is huge.
