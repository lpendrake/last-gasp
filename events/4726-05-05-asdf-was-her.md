# 12 days ago, in  sweaty tavern by the docks

She hustled past a table of drunken sailors, up the stairs at the side, each step creaking like it was one drunkard from retirement, and into the second room.

She didn't knock, there was no time for pleasantries, but that meant she was greeted by the rasp of a sword being drawn, the hum of a wand preparing to fire, and the click of pistol being cocked.

The three people in the room sighed, relaxed and cursed her. They'd all been on edge since... well, no time for that. "Dragan will be here in two days, not four." she tells them, her voice rough, deep for a woman, and she takes off her hammer, thunks it down on the protesting floor-boards, fips a chair round and sits on it in reverse.

Perhaps I should describe our clearly consorting conspirators. We have Flavio, he's the one with the rapier and another pistol, not the one from earlier. He fits right in in this decripit tavern. Despite his name sounding like something from romance play, he's got sunken, dark eyes, a nose like a hawks beak, and eyebrows just bushy enough to notice their bushy. His hair is jet black, and though you'd think him the type to have long greasy hair, it's the one bit he keeps trip and tidy. You see, he's actually a priest of Wandering, a god of adventure and travel, quite popular in these parts.

Then we have Sila Yangeb. She's a nice lady, but again you'd not want her popping out of the shadows in a dark alley. She just gives off this eery vibe. Maybe it's because her face is covered in tattoos. She's a techy, and just a little bit off-kilter. One moment she doesn't seem to remember you exist, the next shes right up in your face, eyes practicaly popping from her skinny face, too many teeth on display for comfort and yammering on about some idea she had last week. Anyway, she's dressed in typically mechanics overalls, but all the pockets are filled with magitech devices. 

Then there's Ogdan. He might be short, but the sheer number of explovies, guns and knives on his person lets anyone looking at his squat "I bend metal bars for fun" body know that if the weapons don't work he'll happily take introduce you to his knuckle dusters. His glorious, very well maintained and oiled mustache is his only redeeming feature.

And finally there's Betty. Technically, ogdan is shorter, but it really is a technicallity. She's got white hair cut in a bob, a slim body entirely at odds with her temperement and weapon of choice, a greathammer, and so absurdely strong and tough that you'd think someone had condensed an giant down into a little woman. As is always the case in this universe, willpower beats logic every time.

"Two days?" exclaims Ogdan. "We can't be ready in two days! We don't even have the shoes yet!"

"And we won't, not until somebody gets me ruby." Sila adds as she sidles up to Flavio in what she probably thinks is a sexy way, but the absurd grin on her face which she thinks makes her look friendly turns the endevour into an act of intimidation worthy of a court case. "Isn't that right, Flavio... Where's my ruby, flavio... Where?" The words tumble out fast and sharp, like a crate rolling down a hill, the cadence all over the place but gaining in inevitable momentum.

"Ok, ok, I'll go check on it again after we're done here." He's used to her by this point. so he barely reacts, though anyone watching closely still sees him flinch a little. He's too cool and staby to react to intimidating crazy women though. Oh yes.

"Ok, ruby for the shoes... what else are we missing?" Asks Ogdan.

"The location he's docking at." Betty answers. "The docks are full for the next three days, he can't dock here."
